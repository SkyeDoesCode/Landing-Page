Why is nuclear enery so unpopular in the modern era?

  • Public Perception and Fear: nucleaar accidents like Chernobyl and Fukushima have significantly impacted public perception of nuclear energy despite significant advances in safety and waste management
  • High Initial Costs: Building Nuclear power plants requires a significant initial investment, moreover, it is very expensive to decommission these statioms.
  • Competition from Renewable energy: The rapid advancement and decreasing costs of renewable energy sources like wind and solar make them very attractive for investors.
  • Political reasons: Political opposition and changes in policy and affect the development of nuclear energy projects. Some governments prioritize more profitable and affordable options such as fossil fuel and renewables due to ideological, political and economic reasons.
  • Regulatory Reasons: The Nuclear industry is HEAVILY regulated, this can affext the development and deployment of new nuclear power plants. The regulatory process is stringent and time-consuming to ensure safety which in-turn adds to the cost and complexity
